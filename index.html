01

A research office built for a region

The R&D Cell coordinates the full research lifecycle across the university's nine faculties.

South Asian University is unusual among research institutions: it answers not to one government but to eight, and its work is judged by what it returns to the region as a whole.

The Research & Development Cell exists to make that ambition practical. It helps faculty design and win sponsored projects, manages intellectual property and technology transfer, and maintains the centralised record of research outcomes that feeds the university's quality assurance.

It also does the quieter work that keeps research moving — negotiating Memoranda of Understanding with partner universities, supporting post-doctoral and fellowship appointments, and convening the conferences and workshops where regional collaborations begin.

The pages that follow set out what that work has produced: where our output is growing, how it is distributed across disciplines, who we work with, and the centres that anchor our long-term programmes.
